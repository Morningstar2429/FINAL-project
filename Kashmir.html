Kashmir Tours
Day 1

Srinagar – Pahalgam

Today we proceed to Pahalgam. En-route we view the Saffron fields at Pampore followed by a visit to Avantipur ruins. Upon arrival we have time to stroll at the Mall road of Pahalgam. In the evening we enjoy a short Meet & Greet session.

Day 2

Sightseeing in Pahalgam

Built by King Avantivarman who founded the Utpala Dynasty, the ruins of Awantipur or Avantipura, located about 29kms from Srinagar, are a silent reminder of the Hindu empire. The temples which are dedicated to Lord Shiva and Vishnu typify the beautiful Kashmiri architecture which was distinct during that period. Over the years, the temple has been reduced to ruins but it is still a popular site. It is a popular place for photography. You can stop to visit this site on the way to Pahalgam. Note: Avantipur ruins visit will be done subject to army permission.
Day 3

Gulmarg to Pahalgam

Location 1 :Apple Valley Visit : Pluck a fresh adventure in Kashmir with a visit to the Apple Valley in Pahalgam Head out from Srinagar and make your way to the beautiful orchards. Enjoy a walk through the lush surroundings, spotting a variety of apples and workers sorting them into batches.

Location 2 :Drive Past Saffron Fields : Fall in love with the purple blossoms of the saffron field in Pampore on your way to Pahalgam. Witness workers picking saffron as you drive past the region, breathing in the aroma of the enchanting spice. Note: A stopover at the field is subject to army permissions. The blooming of saffron is dependent on the season and weather.

Day 4

Sightseeing in Pahalgam

Location 1 :Cricket Bat Factory Visit : Get a glimpse into the making of a cricket bat as you visit the cricket bat factory in Kashmir. Witness the workers cut, chip and shape the willow planks into finished products that are supplied to leading cricketers in the game.

Location 2 :Tour of Aru & Betab Valley : Take in the spectacular views of the beautiful Aru and Betab valley as you head out on a half-day trip. Relax in the lush meadows and spend time at the pristine lakes that you'll will come across on the tour.

Day 5

Pahalgam to Srinagar Sightseeing in Srinagar

Location 1: Nishat Bagh : Nishat Bagh is located on the eastern side of Dal Lake, running along its bank. The word “nishat” literally means joy hence it is also called the Garden of Bliss.

Location 2: Shalimar Garden : It is the largest Mughal garden of all three, stretching along an area of 31 acres on the banks of the Dal Lake. The quintessential feature of the garden is its terraced landscape, with the terraces rising above one another, lined with Chinar trees and beautiful water fountains.
