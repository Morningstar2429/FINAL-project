Mumbai, CSMT Tour

Day 1

Sightseeing in Mumbai


Location 1: Gateway of India :A symbol of Mumbai's spirit, Gateway of India was built in the 20th Century to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary to Mumbai.

Location 2: Marine Drive :Marine Drive is the heart of Mumbai city, geographically and philosophically. Starting from Nariman Point, one can catch a great view of the Black Bay and the Arabian Sea.

Location 3: Hanging Gardens :One of the most popular tourist attractions of Mumbai, the Hanging Gardens is a popular picnic spot among tourists and locals alike.

Day 2

Elephanta Caves

Elephanta Caves, tucked away in a desolate island locally called Gharapuri (city of caves) 10 kilometres east of the Gateway of India. he colonisers identified the structure as a landmark to dock their boats and to also tell it apart from the other smaller islands on the Arabian Sea. it was reassembled by Cadell and Hewett and placed in the Jijamata Udyaan, a zoo and garden in Byculla, Mumbai, where it stands today.

Day 3

Sightseeing in Mumbai

Location 1: Siddhivinayak Temple :Dedicated to the Hindu deity, Lord Ganesha, Siddhivinayak temple was constructed in 1801, by Deubai Patil and Laxman Vithu. Located in Prabhadevi area, it is one of the most beautiful and richest temples situated in Mumbai. The shrine is a unique six-storied structure with a golden dome on the top.

Location 2: Juhu Beach :ne of the most famous Indian beaches, Juhu beach is situated in 'Ville Parle' approximately 18 km from the city center. Known for its peaceful atmosphere and scenic beauty, it serves as a desired location for film shootings.
